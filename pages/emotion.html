Happy

Feeling happy is like when you have a big smile on your face and your heart feels super duper light and full of joy. It's when you're doing something you really love, like playing with your favorite toy, spending time with friends and family, or even enjoying yummy ice cream on a sunny day. Happy feelings can make you want to laugh, dance, and spread kindness to everyone around you!

Angry

Feeling angry is like having a big storm inside you. It's when something happens that makes you feel upset, frustrated, or mad. It might be when someone takes your toy without asking or when things don't go the way you want them to. Your face might get all scrunched up, and you might want to stomp your feet or even yell a little bit. Sometimes, it's tough to control, but just like a storm, it doesn't last forever. It's important to find ways to calm down, like taking deep breaths or talking about how you feel with someone you trust.

Scared

Feeling scared is like when you're watching a really spooky movie, and you suddenly hear a creaky noise in the dark! It's when something makes you feel worried or afraid. Your heart might beat super fast, and you might get butterflies in your tummy. It can happen when you're facing something new or when you imagine something scary. Like when there's a loud thunderstorm or when you think about monsters hiding in your room at night. It's okay to feel scared sometimes! You can feel better by hugging a stuffed animal, talking to someone you trust, or imagining brave superheroes protecting you.

Sorry

Feeling sorry is like when you accidentally spill juice on your friend's favorite book or when you do something that makes someone else sad or upset. It's when you wish you could turn back time and make things better. You might feel a little bit guilty or sad because you didn't mean to do something wrong, but it happened anyway. Saying "I'm sorry" is a way to show that you care about how someone else feels and that you didn't mean to hurt them. It's important to apologize and try to make things right by being kind and helpful.

Gratitude

Feeling gratitude is like having a super special happy feeling inside your heart when someone does something nice for you or when something awesome happens. It's when you feel really, really thankful and happy about it! Like when a friend shares their favorite toy with you or when your parents make your favorite meal. It's a warm, fuzzy feeling that makes you want to say "thank you" and do something nice in return. Gratitude is about noticing and appreciating all the good things around you, big or small, and feeling really happy and thankful for them.
